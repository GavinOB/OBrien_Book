\section{Ellen (O'Brien) Duggan}\label{per:Ellen3OBrien}

\index{O'Brien!Ellen\textsuperscript{3} (1857--1927)|bb}\index{Duggan!Ellen\textsuperscript{3} (O'Brien)|bb} 
\MainPerson{Ellen\textsuperscript{3} O'Brien} (\Lineage{2}{Michael}, \Lineage{1}{William}) was born in Boston, Suffolk County, Massachusetts,\index{Massachusetts!Boston} probably on 16 July 1857.\cite{Ellen3OBrien3Birth} She was likely\footnote{This baptismal record lists parents as Michael O'Brien and Bridget Colman, which could be a misspelling of Colbert. The 1870 census, the first definitive record of Ellen, places her birth between 8 July 1857 and 7 July 1858.} baptized at St.\ John the Baptist Church\index{St. John the Baptist (church)} in Boston (North End)\index{Massachusetts!Boston!North End} on 18 July 1857.\cite{Ellen3OBrien3Baptism} She died in Lynn, Essex County, Massachusetts,\index{Massachusetts!Lynn} on 29 July 1927 and was buried at Holy Cross Cemetery\index{Holy Cross Cemetery} in Malden, Middlesex County, Massachusetts.\cite{Ellen3OBrien3Death}\index{Massachusetts!Malden} Ellen married, at Most Holy Redeemer Church in East Boston\index{Most Holy Redeemer (church)}\index{Massachusetts!Boston!East Boston} on 14 January 1891, \MainPerson{Patrick J.\ Duggan} (aka Durgan).\cite{Ellen3OBrien3Marriage:1}\index{Duggan!Patrick J.|bb}\index{Durgan!Patrick J.|see {Duggan, Patrick J.}} Patrick was born in Ireland\index{Ireland} on February 1862\cite{Census1900Ellen3OBrien3:1} to James Durgan\index{Duggan!James|see {Durgan, James}}\index{Durgan!James} and Catharine (\_\_\_\_\_) Durgan.\index{Durgan!Catharine}{\index{Duggan!Catharine|see {Durgan, Catharine}}\index{_@Unknown surnames!Catharine (mother of Patrick J.\ Duggan)}\cite{Ellen3OBrien3Marriage:2} He died in East Boston\index{Massachusetts!Boston!East Boston} on 6 April 1922 and was buried with Ellen at Holy Cross Cemetery.\index{Holy Cross Cemetery}\cite{PatrickDugganDeath}

In 1880, Ellen was living with her father and stepmother in East Boston\index{Massachusetts!Boston!East Boston} and working at a store.\cite{Census1880Ellen3OBrien3} She married Patrick Duggan, who immigrated from Ireland\index{Ireland} in 1882. He was working as a longshoreman\index{longshoreman} in 1900.\cite{Census1900Ellen3OBrien3} In 1910, Patrick was working as a laborer at a lumber yard,\index{lumber yard} Ellen was a cleaner\index{cleaner} at an office building, and son William was an apprentice at a shoe factory.\cite{Census1910Ellen3OBrien3}\index{shoe factory} In 1920, Patrick and son Edward were both working as teamsters\index{teamster} at trucking companies,\index{trucking company} while Mary was a telephone operator.\cite{Census1920Ellen3OBrien3}\index{telephone company}
